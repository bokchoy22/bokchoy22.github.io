Challenge 5: Accessibility Statement

From the previous challenges, I learned that the website does not have clear indication of location of buttons (such as challenge 1, one must assume it is on the far left). With the chessboard, it is helpful for the visually impaired to know which chess pieces are on what specific space due to the unique html code. Also, the users will know the color of the design due to specified style code. However, since the website reads line by line, it may take a long time for the opponent playing white pieces to decide on their next move. From challenge 3, it is difficult to locate the input bar compared to the label since it's placed underneath rather than next to the label.The button locations are not obvious to the visually impaired so there are problems with accessibility.

In order to address these accessibility concerns, I would include in the html code specific location of the buttons even though they may automatically align to the left. So that the reader may alert the users exactly where the buttons are located. For the chess pieces, I would add a short cut in between the big chess board so that the user may skip to read either the top half or the bottom half instead of waiting for the reader to read the entire board.
